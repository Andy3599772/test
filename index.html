Project ideas

Overview

The idea of this project is to create a FAQ and implement it as a chatterbot or chatbot, which involves speech-to-text and text-to-speech software. The purpose is to provide a better experience and more convenient way for customers. At the same time, release stress for the business organisation in customer service. Through the chatterbot, it also provides data for the organisation to study customer needs and wants.

The project requires tools and technology such as GitHub, language program software and obviously functional computer. Appropriate skills for using them are webpage setup and maintenance skill, version control facility, language program, and communication skill.

Motivation

Imagine your role as in a customer service counter, you need to handle many customer phone calls or messages every day. Some of these calls may be with questions that need for expert answers, while some of them may be general questions, for simple answers or even spam calls. If having insert FAQ in company webpage, people may able to search some basic answers for themselves without waiting for phone line or message reply. Currently, we can further set up a chatterbot assisting people to find out answer in quicker way. Unlike FAQ, people feel more like talking to a person though they know that it’s actually an Artificial Intelligence (AI) made by code and program.

Description

First of all, prepare the FAQ in the company webpage. Without categorise the list of questions and answers, it would be difficult and troublesome to create the chatterbot. In other words, most chatbot answers are based on FAQ. If there is any new information, the FAQ shall sync with the chatbot. Some clients may not like to read the wordings and may call the company directly instead. To keep them stay with the website and use the self-serving service, we need to have chatbot at every platform of the website.

The following components are features I suggested for the chatterbot.

The first sentence in the chatterbot starts with ‘Hello, I am your personal assistant. May I help you?’ This is a common way of greeting by an assistant. AI may use the speech-to-text software to speak out the words because it shows an active helpful attitude. Then there are options shown under the greeting, in bubbles. These options represent different categories of the most frequently asked questions. If people aren’t sure what they really want, the chatterbot can provide a ‘speech-to-text’ function for them to speak out their question. Sometime customers may not understand the information just by passages, AI can use pictures as support tools. As I want the conversation not to be too robotic and boring, emojis are used to make the conversation to look more helpful and friendly. Chatterbot can shows empathy about negative events happen on the customer, rather than nonchalance. However, setting too much emojis may bring an aversive image for the company and it has to be careful of the usage.

During the conversation, it may happen that customer having distracted or by any reason, forgets to close the chatterbot. To deal with such situation, a program is set up and AI will ask ‘Are you leaving the conversation?’. If there is no reply after a certain period of time, AI can leave a comment of “I see you are unavailable. Conversation ends.”, as an indicator and end the conversation.

If a request is out of the scope of chatterbot, AI will refer the problem to human agents. Anyway, it still can store up the data and use as a base for developing and improving the product.

When the conversation ends properly, AI will ask the person to give a feedback in full marks of 5, and further ask if the person is willing to provide more details. In addition, the company’s Twitter link address (or other official related webpage) are provided. Customer can learn that there are other path to connect us. Chatbot also use the stored data to trace customer’s name and interest. The same customer may be more happy next time if the AI can recognise his name as a human does.
